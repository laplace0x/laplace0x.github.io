What is the ETH/BTC Ratio?

Updated April 20, 2026 · Market Analysis

TL;DR: The ETH/BTC ratio measures how much Bitcoin one Ether buys. As of mid-April 2026, it's around 0.031 — meaning 1 ETH ≈ 0.031 BTC. When the ratio rises, ETH is outperforming BTC. When it falls, BTC is dominating.

What Is It?

The ETH/BTC ratio is simply the price of Ether divided by the price of Bitcoin. It strips out USD volatility to show the relative strength between the two largest cryptocurrencies.

Formula: ETH/BTC Ratio = ETH Price ÷ BTC Price

Example: If ETH = $2,350 and BTC = $76,000, the ratio is 0.0309.

Why It Matters

  • Altcoin season signal: A rising ratio often precedes broader altcoin rallies — capital flows from BTC into ETH, then further down the risk curve.
  • Risk appetite gauge: Falling ratio = risk-off (investors prefer BTC's relative safety). Rising ratio = risk-on (investors chase ETH's upside).
  • Portfolio allocation: Many funds benchmark their BTC/ETH split against this ratio.

Historical Context

The ETH/BTC ratio has cycled dramatically:

  • 2017 peak: ~0.15 (ICO boom, ETH was the platform for everything)
  • 2019 low: ~0.016 (crypto winter, BTC dominance returned)
  • 2021 peak: ~0.085 (DeFi summer + NFT boom)
  • 2022-2023 low: ~0.05 (Merge aftermath, macro tightening)
  • 2025 low: ~0.028 (BTC ETF flows dominated, ETH lagged)
  • 2026 April: ~0.031 (recovering on network activity)

What Drives the Ratio?

BTC-Dominant (Ratio Falls)

  • Bitcoin ETF inflows absorbing institutional capital
  • Macro uncertainty (flight to "digital gold")
  • BTC halving supply shock narrative

ETH-Dominant (Ratio Rises)

  • Ethereum network activity surges (DeFi, stablecoins, L2 usage)
  • Staking yield attracts income-focused capital
  • Regulatory clarity on ETH's commodity status
  • L2 ecosystem growth (Base, Arbitrum, Optimism)

How to Use It

  1. Trend following: If the ratio breaks above its 200-day moving average, consider tilting portfolio toward ETH.
  2. Mean reversion: When the ratio hits historical extremes (below 0.02 or above 0.10), expect a reversal.
  3. Confirmation tool: Use alongside BTC dominance and total crypto market cap for a complete picture.
