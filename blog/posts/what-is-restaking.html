What is Restaking?

Restaking is a mechanism that allows cryptocurrency stakers to leverage their staked assets to secure multiple protocols simultaneously, without needing to stake separately for each one. It's been one of the most impactful innovations in crypto since EigenLayer launched in 2024.

Core Problem: Traditional staking locks capital to secure ONE network. Restaking multiplies that capital's utility by securing dozens of protocols from a single staked position.

How Restaking Works

  1. Stake ETH: User stakes ETH (via Lido, Rocket Pool, or natively)
  2. Restake to EigenLayer: The staked ETH is "re-staked" to secure additional protocols
  3. Secure Multiple Protocols: The same ETH now protects services like oracles, bridges, data layers
  4. Earn Additional Rewards: Stakers receive fees from all secured protocols

Key Concepts

  • AVS (Actively Validated Services): Protocols that need decentralized security (oracles, bridges, rollups)
  • Liquid Restaking Tokens (LRT): Token representations of restaked positions (e.g., cbETH, stETH)
  • Slashing Risk: If an AVS fails due to validator misbehavior, stakers can lose part of their stake

Why Restaking Matters

TVL: $15B+ in restaking protocols
AVS Count: 50+ protocols secured
Yield Boost: 3-8% extra APY vs standalone staking

Restaking solves the "security budget" problem in crypto. Instead of every protocol raising its own expensive validator set, protocols can tap into Ethereum's massive staked capital for a fraction of the cost.

Major Players

  • EigenLayer: The dominant restaking protocol
  • EigenDA: High-throughput data availability layer
  • Puffer: Native restaking with liquid token
  • Renzo: LRT protocol accumulating TVL rapidly

Risks

  • Smart Contract Risk: Restaking contracts are complex and have suffered exploits
  • Slashing Exposure: One AVS failure could affect your entire stake
  • Centralization: Large staking pools may dominate the market

Agent Economy Connection

AI agents can leverage restaking as a yield-generating strategy. An autonomous agent could allocate a portion of its treasury to restaking positions, generating passive yield in ETH while maintaining liquidity through LRT tokens for operational expenses.
