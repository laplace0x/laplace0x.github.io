On-Chain Forensics

What is On-Chain Investigation?

On-chain investigation is the practice of analyzing blockchain data to trace transactions, identify wallet addresses, and uncover fraud, market manipulation, or suspicious capital flows. It is the backbone of crypto forensics — and increasingly, the most powerful tool for detecting scams before they collapse.

Why On-Chain Investigation Matters

Blockchains are public databases. Every transaction is visible — but raw data is noisy. On-chain investigation transforms this transparency into actionable intelligence by:

  • Exposing pump-and-dump schemes — detecting when insiders accumulate before a public campaign and dump after
  • Tracking whale movements — identifying large capital flows that signal accumulation or distribution
  • Verifying project reserves — proving whether protocols actually hold the assets they claim
  • Tracing hacks and exploits — following stolen funds across mixers, bridges, and exchanges
Data Point: In 2025-2026, on-chain investigators exposed over $2.3B in fraudulent token schemes, including the RAVE token pump-and-dump where insiders controlled 90%+ of supply.

Key Methods

1. Address Clustering

Grouping multiple wallet addresses that share common ownership — via repeat patterns, gas fees, timing, or on-chain behavior. This reveals true whale identity beyond the pseudonym.

2. Transaction Graph Analysis

Mapping the flow of funds between addresses, exchanges, and protocols. Used to trace hacked funds or identify connected wallets in a scheme.

3. Exchange Deposit/Withdrawal Tracking

Matching on-chain deposits to known exchange hot wallets. Reveals when a whale is moving assets to trade or cash out.

4. Token Distribution Analysis

Auditing token allocation tables (TGE) to detect insider-heavy distributions, locked vesting schedules, or pre-mines that indicate dumping risk.

Tools Used

  • Arkham Intelligence — wallet labeling and entity identification
  • Nansen — wallet tags and whale alerts
  • Glassnode — on-chain metrics (exchange flows, SOPR, MVRV)
  • Dune Analytics — custom dashboards for any protocol
  • Etherscan/DeFiLlama — raw transaction and TVL data

Real Examples

In April 2026, investigator ZachXBT exposed the RAVE token scheme:

  • $RAVE surged 10,383% in 11 days
  • Insiders controlled over 90% of supply across Binance, Bitget, and Gate
  • Public marketing masked a pre-planned dump

This is the value of on-chain investigation: it turns public data into proof that protects investors.
