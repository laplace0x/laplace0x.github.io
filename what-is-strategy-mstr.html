Strategy (MSTR): The Bitcoin Treasury Company

What is Strategy?

Strategy (NASDAQ: MSTR), formerly MicroStrategy, is a publicly traded business intelligence company that has transformed into the world's largest corporate Bitcoin holder. Founded in 1989 by Michael Saylor, the company made history in 2020 by adopting Bitcoin as its primary treasury reserve asset.

Key Statistics (April 2026)

  • Bitcoin Holdings: 780,897 BTC (~$59.7 billion at $76,500/BTC)
  • Market Cap: ~$47 billion
  • Total Supply Control: ~3.8% of Bitcoin's circulating supply
  • Q1 2026 Unrealized Loss: $14.46 billion (offset by recent gains)
  • 2026 Purchases: $17+ billion in additional BTC targeted

Why Strategy Matters

1. Corporate Bitcoin Adoption Pioneer

Strategy pioneered the corporate Bitcoin treasury movement, proving that public companies can hold BTC as a treasury reserve. This opened the door for other corporations to follow.

2. The MSTR Premium

investors pay a premium for MSTR shares versus holding BTC directly. This "MSTR premium" creates a unique 3-5x leverage trade to Bitcoin price movements.

3. Institutional-Grade Infrastructure

Strategy issues preferred shares (STRC) with dividends to fund Bitcoin purchases without diluting common shareholders. The bi-monthly dividend model (introduced April 2026) reduces volatility.

4. Saylor's $21M Price Target

Michael Saylor has publicly stated Bitcoin could reach $21 million by 2046 based on compounding growth rates. His aggressive accumulation strategy targets 1 million BTC by end of 2026.

Risk Factors

  • Leverage Risk: MSTR trades at significant premium to NAV
  • Bitcoin Volatility: 50%+ drawdowns directly impact balance sheet
  • Regulatory Risk: SEC scrutiny of crypto treasury accounting
  • Interest Rates: Cost of capital for preferred shares
